# Sales playbook — agentic cloud onboarding & management

Positioning, target customer profiles, and sales pitches for the agentic
cloud-transformation service. Companion to the pricing model:
**onboarding fee + 15% of managed cloud spend (tiered, monthly minimum)**.

---

## The one-sentence position

> **"Enterprise-grade cloud foundations at SMB prices, delivered in a day,
> fully transparent, zero lock-in."**

Every clause is something a traditional MSP's business model actively
prevents them from offering:

| Clause | Why MSPs can't say it |
|---|---|
| Enterprise-grade foundations | Their SMB tier gets junior engineers and templates from 2019 |
| SMB prices | Their onboarding costs them $10-30k in labor; minimums start ~$1,500/mo |
| Delivered in a day | Their process is a 4-8 week SOW with discovery workshops |
| Fully transparent | Their work happens in tickets; customers see invoices, not diffs |
| Zero lock-in | Their retention model depends on undocumented environments |

## Target customer profiles (the gap MSPs leave open)

### Profile 1 — The graduating startup
- **Who:** 5-25 headcount, SaaS or services, $0-2k/mo current cloud spend
  (often on a founder's credit card, default settings, no governance).
- **Trigger:** first enterprise customer asks for a security questionnaire;
  first SOC 2 conversation; investor due diligence.
- **Why MSPs ignore them:** spend too small to justify onboarding cost.
- **What they buy:** credibility. A governed landing zone they can show
  a customer's security team.

### Profile 2 — The Microsoft-shop SMB going to cloud
- **Who:** 20-100 headcount, traditional business (professional services,
  light manufacturing, healthcare-adjacent), already on M365, servers
  in a closet or a local datacenter contract expiring.
- **Trigger:** hardware refresh cycle, office move, ransomware scare,
  "our IT guy retired."
- **Why MSPs ignore them:** they get quoted a migration SOW they can't
  afford, so they stall for another year.
- **What they buy:** a safe, guided path to Azure that doesn't require
  hiring anyone.

### Profile 3 — The under-served MSP refugee
- **Who:** 30-150 headcount, already in the cloud, $3-15k/mo spend,
  currently with a mid-tier MSP.
- **Trigger:** opaque invoices, slow ticket turnaround, a failed audit
  finding, discovering they don't own their own environment's docs.
- **Why MSPs lose them:** opacity and lock-in are the incumbent's
  business model, not a fixable bug.
- **What they buy:** transparency and an exit ramp. Everything in their
  own repo, every change a reviewed plan.

### Profile 4 — The agency / dev shop with client clouds
- **Who:** 5-30 headcount digital agencies and dev shops that spin up
  client environments ad hoc and dread managing them.
- **Trigger:** a client environment breaks; a client asks who's
  responsible for security; too many subscriptions, no standards.
- **Why MSPs ignore them:** many small environments = many small
  onboardings = economically dead for a labor-based model. For an
  agent, N environments is a loop.
- **What they buy:** a white-glove foundation per client without
  hiring an infra person.

## Universal objection handlers

- **"How is this cheaper than an MSP?"** — Their 15% pays for humans on
  retainer; ours pays for software. Our cost to onboard you is an agent
  run, not three weeks of an architect's calendar. Same margin math,
  different cost structure — the savings are structural, not a
  promotional discount.
- **"Is AI making changes to my cloud?"** — The AI *proposes*; nothing
  applies without an approved plan. You see the exact change — the same
  diff a senior engineer would review — before it happens. That's more
  oversight than a ticket queue gives you, not less.
- **"What if we outgrow you?"** — Then you graduate with everything:
  your tenant, your IaC repo, your documentation. A clean handoff to an
  enterprise MSP is a feature we're proud of, and the reason customers
  trust us with the years before that.
- **"Why should optimization advice come from someone paid on our
  spend?"** — Every recommendation ships with its cost impact stated
  up front, including the ones that cut our fee. Tiered pricing means
  we grow on your success, not your waste — and the audit trail proves it.

## Where we don't compete (disqualify fast, refer gracefully)

- Needs 24/7 human incident response with contractual SLAs.
- Needs a HIPAA BAA / FedRAMP / formal compliance attestation today.
- Enterprise procurement with vendor-risk processes longer than our
  delivery time.

A graceful referral to a large MSP costs one deal and buys a
reputation. The handoff-ready repo makes us the easiest vendor they
will ever replace — say that out loud in the sales call.
